The Curious Case of South Africa's Income Inequality




This is a selection of countries with a Gini coefficient of 70 or above as recorded by the World Income Inequality Database. All countries happen to be African countries, with South Africa being the most recent to join the ranks of the highest income inequality in the world. All of these nations have had a significant series of political and social turbulence around the time of their entering this Club. It is unclear what significant events led up to South Africa's debut in this group.
